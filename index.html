Cubits

In Semester 1 of 2018, I produced a prototype for a workforce management system.

Cubits is a physical workforce management system in which tangible cubes represent tasks within an agile work environment.
I conducted literature reviews, user observations with Redback Technologies employees, and developed the accompanying website demonstrating the product.
The product was well received at the University of Queensland Interaction Design exhibit.

TeleDR

In November 2016, I dipped my toes into usability research.

As part of the University of Queensland's Summer Research Program, I produced a UX research paper detailing recommendations for an online GP refferral system.
Reported to TeleDR, a telehealth company looking to explore the problem space of developing an online GP service.
I conducted literature reviews, competitor analyses, and developing wireframes based on prior research.

DePicture

In August 2016, I participated in a group project dedicated to conducting research on abstract reasoning.

Very little is known about how the human brain processes abstract art. What are interpretations? How do they differ amongst other people?
DePicture is a web application designed to collect data for exploratory research. In a team of 4, I conceptualised the design and functionality of this web-based application.
As my first large group project, I learnt the importance of collaboration, time management, and usability testing.
